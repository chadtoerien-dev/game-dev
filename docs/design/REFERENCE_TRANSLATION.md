# Reference Translation and Originality Framework

## Principle

Reference games define useful design problems and quality bars. They do not define the identity of **The Veil**.

The project may draw from broad concepts such as systemic stealth, schedules, contextual access and readable alert states, but it must use original setting, characters, architecture, dialogue, UI, missions, score and presentation.

## Central translation: socially engineered vulnerability

The player should not merely discover existing elimination opportunities. The player creates them through the social simulation.

```text
knowledge
→ social leverage
→ access or isolation
→ preparation
→ execution opportunity
→ evidence
→ escape or concealment
→ social consequence
```

Example:

```text
learn that a contestant fears accusation
→ earn their trust
→ promise private evidence
→ arrange an archive meeting
→ persuade them to dismiss an ally
→ gain night access
→ leave a social trail proving that the meeting was arranged
```

The opportunity creates vulnerability and evidence simultaneously.

## Social stealth

Access is not a global Boolean. An observer evaluates:

- Perceived identity
- Clothing or role
- Invitation
- Relationship
- Carried equipment
- Time
- Area security
- Behaviour
- Known events
- Observer knowledge

Possible outputs:

- Authorised
- Tolerated
- Unusual
- Questionable
- Trespassing
- Hostile

A social explanation may stop immediate escalation while still creating a memory that later becomes a contradiction.

## Disguises

The slice should prove only two limited concealment types:

- Staff outerwear for service-area plausibility
- Borrowed formal cloak or coat for distance concealment

Compromise is observer-specific. Witness records include distance, lighting, duration, clothing, body-shape confidence, facial recognition and knowledge of the garment owner.

## Dynamic routines

Contestants have plans, not fixed patrol routes.

Routine inputs include:

- Phase requirements
- Relationships
- Fear and suspicion
- Protection
- Injury
- Threats
- Promises
- Challenge responsibilities
- Private objectives
- Known routes
- Access and safety

Changes must be discoverable through conversation, observation, surveillance, written schedules, allies or visible behaviour. The game must not secretly move targets solely to defeat the player.

## Opportunity Threads

An Opportunity Thread identifies a possible causal chain, not a compulsory scripted story.

Data includes:

- Trigger knowledge
- Relevant contestants
- Required relationship or access
- Preparation actions
- Operation methods
- Likely witnesses
- Possible evidence
- Expiry and risk

States:

- Unknown
- Hinted
- Understood
- Prepared
- Available
- Compromised
- Completed
- Abandoned
- Expired

Assistance modes range from journal-only to optional directed markers.

## Environmental operations

Every hazard needs:

- Normal function
- Manipulation method
- Preparation state
- Trigger
- Failure state
- Witness possibilities
- Physical evidence
- Interpretation
- Concealment or restoration option

The archive slice supports socially engineered entry, secret-route entry, mechanical sabotage and contamination.

## Evidence generation

Every operation method calls a common consequence path:

```text
operation action
→ physical events
→ perception events
→ evidence records
→ witness memories
→ information transmission
→ suspicion interpretation
```

Evidence stores origin, location, timestamp, integrity, contamination, reliability, discoverability, possible owners, interpretations, related witnesses, planting state and observer belief.

A clean operation leaves evidence that is weak, ambiguous, misleading or difficult to connect to the player.

## Security escalation

Original awareness ladder:

```text
Unaware
→ Noticed
→ Curious
→ Observing
→ Investigating
→ Challenging
→ Alarmed
→ Searching
→ Pursuing or Lockdown
→ Aftermath
```

Observers communicate uncertain identity, clothing, location, direction, behaviour, time, access method, carried item and threat level. Reports do not transmit perfect AI knowledge.

## Light and sound

Detection considers distance, angle, exposure, luminance, contrast, speed, stance, cover, alertness and observation duration.

Noise events include location, loudness, category, duration, instigator, surface, occlusion and plausible social explanation.

The estate must remain readable. Darkness cannot be used to hide weak visuals.

## Tactical camera: Survey Mode

Survey Mode organises earned information. It does not grant omniscience.

It may display known routes, last-known positions, heard sounds, discovered restricted zones, access points, secret passages, placed equipment and estimated danger.

It must not show unknown NPCs through walls, objective intentions, undiscovered evidence or numerical guilt.

The slice initially supports target marking, one route-marker chain, known sound sources, discovered secret route and placed equipment.

## Cinematic direction

Three camera modes:

- **Social intimacy:** eye-level framing, controlled close-ups, negative space and long holds.
- **Institutional ritual:** symmetry, wide architecture, slow tracks, formal entrances and silhouettes.
- **Escalating danger:** closer tracking, faster but readable cutting and reduced symmetry.

Sequencer may present outcomes but must not determine them.

Avoid constant handheld movement, rapid cutting, excessive depth of field, unreadable darkness and cinematic teleportation.

## Replayability

Replayability comes from recombination of:

- Player faction
- Veiled identities
- Starting relationships
- Personality weights
- Private objectives
- Initial rumours
- Challenge teams
- Reward recipients
- Opportunity availability
- Secret-route knowledge
- Security arrangements
- Fear responses
- Evidence and witness schedules
- Voting commitments

No approach should remain optimal across all social configurations.
