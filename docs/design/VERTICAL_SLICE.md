# Vertical Slice Definition

## Proof question

Can physical action, social relationships, information provenance, evidence and voting combine into an enjoyable 25–30-minute round?

Everything not required to answer this question is deferred.

## Target experience

- Single-player
- Windows PC
- Third-person camera
- 25–30 minutes
- Eight contestants
- Two Veiled
- Six Loyal
- Player role selected or randomly assigned
- One complete round plus the consequences at the next Dawn Assembly
- Three meaningfully different runs before content exhaustion

## Round structure and target timing

| Phase | Target | Purpose |
|---|---:|---|
| Dawn Assembly | 2 minutes | Reveal consequences, observe reactions and establish theories |
| Social free-roam | 6 minutes | Build relationships, gather information, eavesdrop and prepare opportunities |
| Reliquary Run challenge | 5 minutes | Test movement, cooperation, competition and optional sabotage |
| Pre-vote lobbying | 3 minutes | Convert information into promises and political blocs |
| Tribunal | 5 minutes | Present claims, challenge contradictions and defend accusations |
| Vote and Exile | 2 minutes | Resolve the political phase and persist consequences |
| Night planning | 1–2 minutes | Choose target, protection, equipment or interception plan |
| Night operation | 5 minutes | Execute, prevent or observe the archive operation |
| Aftermath | 2 minutes | Resolve evidence, witnesses and the following Dawn Assembly |

## Environment

Build one compact, connected manor wing containing:

- Entrance hall
- Dining room
- Drawing room
- Library
- Tribunal chamber
- Guest corridor
- Four accessible bedrooms
- Kitchen
- Servants’ passage
- Courtyard
- Underground storage chamber
- One secret passage
- One secured archive

Do not build the full estate.

The approved architectural basis is King’s College, Cambridge. Reconstruct the selected footprint at one-to-one spatial scale and maximum source-verified fidelity, following `docs/design/KINGS_COLLEGE_CAMBRIDGE_REPLICA_BRIEF.md`. This does not expand the vertical slice to the full College.

Any room, connection or decorative element that cannot be supported by authorised measurements or sources must be marked as an authored production interpretation. The Veil’s fiction, characters, missions, UI, audio and protected expression remain original.

## Challenge: The Reliquary Run

Contestants split into two teams and recover three reliquary pieces from the courtyard, servants’ route and underground chamber.

The challenge proves:

- Responsive running, turning, jumping and vaulting
- Route choice
- Object carrying and delivery
- Basic AI cooperation
- Social reactions to success and failure
- Optional sabotage
- A Ward reward
- A limited voting advantage

Player decisions include helping a teammate, sharing a shortcut, concealing information, misdirecting a rival, asking for carrying assistance and sacrificing personal reward for an ally.

The challenge must reuse exploration and night-operation mechanics rather than becoming an isolated minigame framework.

## Night scenario: secured archive

### Veiled player

The player enters or manipulates the archive wing and compromises a target while avoiding a guard and another contestant.

Slice approaches:

1. Socially engineered private meeting
2. Secret-passage entry
3. Sabotaged archive mechanism
4. Contaminated drink or medicine

Direct confrontation is possible but highly risky. Firearms and long-range attacks are excluded.

### Loyal player

The same location supports:

- Guarding the target
- Following a suspect
- Placing a sound trap
- Watching the secret route
- Searching the archive
- Interrupting an operation
- Allowing suspicious activity to continue to gather stronger evidence

## Required persistent consequences

- Accusations and defences
- Voting history
- Challenge performance
- Challenge sabotage suspicion
- Alliance promises
- Broken promises
- Evidence discovered, concealed or planted
- Witnessed night actions
- Physical traces
- Night objective success or failure
- Exile correctness
- Credibility changes
