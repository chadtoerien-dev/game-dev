Contestants complete a playable mission that affects the prize fund and social relationships.

Challenges should require:

- Movement
- Cooperation
- Exploration
- Risk
- Decision-making
- Resource allocation
- Possible sabotage
- Personal sacrifice
- Competition between teams

Challenge rewards may include provisional versions of:

- Prize money
- Protection
- An additional vote
- A secret key
- Private evidence
- Access to restricted areas
- A future favour
- Challenge advantages
- Night equipment

Any terminology too close to an existing television format should be treated as placeholder terminology.

4. Pre-vote strategy

The player has limited time to influence the vote.

Possible actions include:

- Build a voting bloc
- Persuade undecided contestants
- Leak evidence
- Plant false evidence
- Defend an ally
- Betray an ally
- Pressure a contestant
- Stage a confrontation
- Expose a contradiction
- Promise future support
- Change the target of the vote

5. Round-table debate

This must be interactive and systemic.

The player should be able to:

- Speak early
- Stay quiet
- Interrupt
- Answer accusations
- Ask direct questions
- Support another accusation
- Defend someone
- Reveal evidence
- Withhold evidence
- Lie
- Change position
- Challenge inconsistencies
- Make a closing argument

NPCs should react to:

- Evidence
- Credibility
- Trust
- Fear
- Group pressure
- Existing alliances
- Personality
- Previous voting history
- Their own strategic interests

6. Vote and banishment

Every contestant privately votes.

The selected contestant leaves and their faction may be revealed, depending on the selected game mode.

Consequences should persist:

- Correct accusations improve credibility
- Incorrect accusations damage credibility
- Betrayals damage relationships
- Quiet players may become suspicious
- Aggressive players may become targets
- Voting blocs may strengthen or fracture
- A conspirator may help remove another conspirator to protect themselves

7. Night phase

If the player is one of the Veiled:

- Attend the secret conclave
- Propose a target
- Persuade the other conspirator
- Select an approach
- Select equipment
- Decide who performs the operation
- Decide whether to eliminate, recruit, intimidate or frame
- Execute a real third-person stealth operation
- Escape without leaving decisive evidence

Potential elimination approaches include:

- Silent close-range attack
- Poison
- Environmental trap
- Sabotaged security
- Disguised entry
- Secret passage
- Long-range approach
- Manipulating the target into leaving safety
- Framing another contestant
- Creating a distraction

If the player is Loyal:

- Protect an ally
- Follow a suspect
- Search a bedroom
- Install surveillance
- Investigate hidden routes
- Set a trap
- Recover evidence
- Intercept a night operation
- Survive an attempted attack
- Use protection as bait
- Guard a vulnerable contestant

A failed night operation should create consequences rather than always ending the game immediately.

PLAYER SOCIAL ARCHETYPES

Support blended character development rather than fixed classes.

1. The Ghost

- Low visibility
- Strong stealth
- Strong private conversations
- Less likely to be targeted
- Lower public influence
- May be accused of contributing nothing

2. The Commander

- Strong leadership
- Strong public persuasion
- Builds voting blocs
- Performs well in challenges
- Becomes a high-value target
- Suffers heavily after incorrect accusations

3. The Charmer

- Builds affinity quickly
- Gains private information
- Defuses accusations
- Creates social protection
- Becomes vulnerable to relationship obligations and betrayal

4. The Detective

- Recognises contradictions
- Evaluates evidence
- Unlocks investigative dialogue
- Identifies staged scenes
- Becomes dangerous to conspirators

5. The Manipulator

- Redirects suspicion
- Spreads rumours
- Creates conflict
- Performs well as a conspirator
- Risks catastrophic exposure if contradictions accumulate

6. The Enforcer

- Uses intimidation
- Performs strongly in physical missions
- Protects allies
- Can dominate confrontations
- May create fear without genuine loyalty

NPC REQUIREMENTS

NPCs are the most important system in the game.

Each contestant should have:

- Public persona
- Hidden personality traits
- Social strategy
- Personal objectives
- Risk tolerance
- Loyalty threshold
- Fear response
- Preferred communication style
- Friends
- Rivals
- Attraction or affinity where appropriate
- Private suspicions
- Evidence knowledge
- Rumour knowledge
- Voting intention
- Memory of meaningful events
- Ability to lie
- Ability to misinterpret evidence
- Ability to change their mind
- Ability to communicate while the player is absent

NPCs must maintain opinions of one another, not merely opinions of the player.

Suggested relationship values include:

- Trust
- Suspicion
- Affinity
- Respect
- Fear
- Loyalty
- Resentment
- Perceived competence
- Perceived honesty
- Influence
- Credibility

NPC decision-making should combine:

- Personality
- Current evidence
- Information provenance
- Relationships
- Group pressure
- Personal survival
- Strategic advantage
- Role-specific goals

VOTING MODEL

Create a systemic voting model.

A contestant’s likelihood of voting for a candidate may consider:

- Personal suspicion
- Evidence strength
- Evidence reliability
- Social pressure
- Fear
- Rivalry
- Perceived threat
- Trust
- Loyalty
- Credibility of accusers
- Voting-bloc commitments
- Candidate influence
- Self-preservation

Different personalities must weight these factors differently.

Avoid predetermined scripted voting unless required for a specific tutorial or narrative sequence.
